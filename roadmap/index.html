The road to a website you love!

Designing a website is a partnership between the client and the designer.

Communication is essential. TeXariti needs to know what goals you have for your organization. What message you want your website to send? What do you want to convey to your customer or prospect?

To help build communication, we will follow a few well-defined steps.

The best way to build something unique is to do it in stages. Designers call it “Agile development.”

  • Step 1. Lets say you want a static, great-looking landing page first.
  • Step 2. Then you want blog-posting ability on a Word Press engine.
  • Step 3. Next you want a newsletter designed and formatted.
  • Step 4. You want a mobile version of the newsletter.
  • Step 5. Next you want a Shopify store added into your website.

This lets the client make decisions as they go and adapt to what is working and what isn't just right.

Defining steps also allow the client pay for the project in stages. You only pay for what TeXariti is currently doing. There is no long-term lock-in. You own the website and all code behind it.

I'm interested! I have a lot of questions. How do we begin?

1. First conversation

In our first conversation you can share with us what kind of website you are thinking you need.

Once we get a feel for what you want your website to be, we can give you a general cost that you may be looking at.

I know I need a good website, but it all seems so confusing. How can I know what I need?

2. Informal chat

We can meet in person, over the phone or by internet chat. If you are in the Oklahoma City area we can meet at your convenience. We will talk with you about your business or your church and what you are wanting from a web project.

We will talk about you, who you are.

  • What are your goals?
  • What is your business/church about?
  • Are you a business? What do you sell?
  • What do you want your web visitors to know about you?
  • What impression do you want your visitors to feel about you?
  • Who is your target customer or unreached group?
  • What are you doing to reach them now?

TeXariti will put together a plan for your website. By determining the requirements you have. TeXariti will also have suggestions about how to make your online presence the most effective it can be.

One helpful thing we will ask is to think of what websites you think look good to you. Knowing what you like in other organization's websites will help us understand what you may be wanting for your site.

When do I find out how much this is going to cost?

3. Proposal and estimates

A great website isn't a cost, it is an investment. Whether you are a for-profit business or a non-profit organization, a beautiful, effective web presence will bring in new customers and members.

After we have arrived at a list of project goals, TeXariti will compose an estimate that includes clear milestones.

In this process, together we will decide on the scope of the project. We will have a list that specifies what TeXariti, as the developer, will do for you. We will also have a list that specifies what you as a client will do (like what content or photos you may provide). We will also agree on what the project scope does not include. By setting expectations early we will be able to have a productive partnership.

Who is going to work on my project?

4. Meet the Team

We will go over the plans together with the team that will work on your project. We want to be sure to clearly understand what you are needing and expecting from your website. We also will have many suggestions on how to expand your business or organization with an effective web presence.

We will also answer any questions you have. We want you to be pleased with every step in the process!

How is my website going to work?

5. Graybox Prototype!

Many designers like to lay out a website using gray boxes and dummy text before they work on the actual design. It is the web-design equivalent of sketching a design on paper.

We will make a clickable, working website framework for you. It won't be pretty yet, but it will help you get a feel for how your website will work.

Sounds good. When do I make the decision to move forward?

6. Project Confirmation

Once you are satisfied with the project plans and we agree on an estimate, we can get started. If the project scope requires it, we will exchange contracts and accept a deposit.

When do you need my content and photos? What if I have content, but I'm not really happy with it? Can you help?

7. Content

Find your message and objectives

We can help! The content of your website is the most important aspect to the success of your project. TeXariti can help you compose the language for your website. TeXariti will need your content in at least a rough draft form before TeXariti can begin work on the design. We can work together to find just the right verbage for your site.

Photos

Photos will make or break a website! Most websites have photos that are far too small. The photos don't have to be perfect. Emotional impact is not based on photo quality, it comes from what is in the photo.

At this stage it will be time for you to supply photos, if applicable. The more photos to choose from the better. We can improve imperfect photos.

How long will my project take to finish?

8. We Design Your Website!

The length of time it takes to finish your web project depends on how much content you have and how detailed the design is going to be. It also depends on the time it takes our clients to respond to our communications. Building an excellent website is a partnership... it takes a lot of talking!

Some projects can be completed in just a couple of weeks; large projects may take a few months. The good news is that large projects can be done in stages, so it doesn't take months to have something beautiful online.

When do I get to see my website?

9. Testing and Review

When an author writes a book the first draft is never the final version! It takes time to refine, to shape, to phrase and re-phrase. Crafting a quality website can't be rushed. We will work with you, going over every detail, so that your website is the best that it can be!

I can't wait! I'm excited!

10. Launch!

Your new website is finished! Hours of work and careful attention to detail have come to an end, and the project is ready to go public. You will be able to study your completed website and let us know of any tiny details that need to be addressed. Once you are completely satisfied, we will upload your website to the host of your choice. Your website is viewable by the world!

What about after the launch? How will I know my website is as effective as it can be?

11. Performance Reviews

We consider our clients to be our long-term partners, making sure that they have the best web experience possible. We won't leave you hanging!

If you want a great website, you don't just make a design and walk away and leave it sitting.

  • Is this design the best it can be in drawing visitors?
  • Is this design effective in converting visitors to customers?
  • How will success be measured?
  • What are the real-world results?
  • Are you, our client, still excited with your website? Are you proud to show it off to your friends?
  • What can we learn from your visitor statistics? Where are most of your visits coming from?

Every so often we will conduct a performance review of your site. We want to make sure your website is the most effective tool it can be to promote your business or organization!

I'm so happy! I love my new website, and everyone says how pretty it is!

This is the part where you tell everyone you know about us.
